CHUNK 1: WHAT ARE DESIGN CRITERIA?

TEACHER CHUNK
10 mins

Understanding Design Criteria

Design criteria are a list of 'must have' features or requirements that you need to consider when making your project. They help you focus on what's important and guide your design decisions.

1

What Are Design Criteria?

Design criteria are specific statements that describe what your project must do or have to be successful. They are like a checklist for your project!

Good design criteria are specific, measurable, and achievable.

2

Why Do We Need Them?

Design criteria help you:

  • Stay focused on what's important
  • Make decisions when you're not sure what to do
  • Check if your project is on track
  • Know when your project is complete and successful
3

Examples of Design Criteria

Here are some examples of design criteria for different projects:

Project Type Example Design Criteria
Card Game
  • The game must be playable by 2-4 players
  • A game should last 15-20 minutes
  • Rules must fit on a single page
  • The game should be fun and engaging
School Website
  • The website must work on phones and computers
  • Users must find information in 3 clicks or less
  • Text must be easy to read (large enough font)
  • colours must match school branding
Science Presentation
  • Must include at least 3 scientific facts
  • Should last between 3-5 minutes
  • Must include visual aids (diagrams, pictures)
  • Language should be appropriate for Year 6 audience
4

Writing Good Design Criteria

When writing design criteria, make sure they are:

SPECIFIC

Not: "My project should look good"
But: "My project must use at least 3 different colours that contrast well"

MEASURABLE

Not: "My biography should have lots of information"
But: "My biography must include at least 5 key facts about the person"

ACHIEVABLE

Not: "My project will have 50 interactive elements"
But: "My project will have at least 1 interactive element per cube face"

    CHUNK 2: MERGE CUBE DESIGN CRITERIA

    TEACHER CHUNK
    10 mins

    Designing for Merge Cubes

    Merge Cubes are special because they have 6 sides that can display different content. When designing for Merge Cubes, we need specific criteria that address this unique format.

    1

    Understanding Merge Cubes

    A Merge Cube has 6 sides (faces) that can display different content when viewed through an app or website:

    • Front face
    • Back face
    • Left face
    • Right face
    • Top face
    • Bottom face

    When creating a biographical project, each face can tell a different part of the person's story!

    FRONT
    Early Life
    BACK
    Later Years
    RIGHT
    Achievements
    LEFT
    Challenges
    TOP
    Legacy
    BOTTOM
    Timeline
    2

    Merge Cube Design Considerations

    When creating design criteria for a Merge Cube project, consider these important factors:

    CONTENT ORGANIZATION

    How will you divide content across the 6 faces? Each face should have a clear purpose.

    NAVIGATION

    How will users know which face to look at next? Consider using arrows or numbering.

    VISUAL DESIGN

    Will all faces share a common theme? What colours, fonts, and images will you use?

    INTERACTIVITY

    What interactive elements will you include? Buttons, animations, or pop-ups?

    TEXT AMOUNT

    How much text will you include on each face? Too much text can be hard to read.

    USABILITY

    How easy is it for someone to use your cube? Consider testing with others.

    2

    Design Criteria Reflection

    Now that you've planned your cube faces, take some time to reflect on your design criteria:

    Will my current face planning fulfill all my design criteria?

    Are there any design criteria I might struggle to meet with my current plan?

    Do I need to adjust any of my criteria, or modify my cube face plan?

    What might be the most challenging criterion to meet, and why?

    Remember: It's fine to adjust your design criteria or your face planning at this stage. The important thing is that they work well together!

    3

    Think-Pair-Share: Design Feedback

    THINK

    Spend 2 minutes reviewing your own design criteria and cube face plan.

    • What are you most confident about?
    • What aspects might need improvement?
    • What questions do you have?
    PAIR

    Share your plan with a partner (5 minutes).

    Ask each other these questions:

    • Do you think my design will meet all my criteria?
    • Is my content organization logical?
    • What's the strongest part of my design?
    • What could I improve?
    SHARE

    Reflect on your partner's feedback.

    Consider these points:

    • What feedback did you find most helpful?
    • What changes might you make to your design?
    • What did you learn from seeing someone else's approach?
    Make any changes you think are necessary in your assessment slides
