CHUNK 1: WHAT MAKES A PASSWORD STRONG?

TEACHER CHUNK
10 mins

Building Unbreakable Digital Locks

Your password is like a lock on your digital life. Just like you wouldn't use a flimsy lock on your bike, you shouldn't use a weak password on your accounts. Let's learn what makes a password strong enough to keep hackers out!

1

Password Strength Ingredients

What Makes Passwords Strong

Length (12+ characters)

Longer passwords are exponentially harder to crack

Mix of Characters

Uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols

Unpredictable

Not based on personal information

Unique

Different for every account

What Makes Passwords Weak

Personal Information

Birthdays, names, addresses

Common Patterns

123456, password, qwerty

Too Short

Less than 8 characters

Reused Everywhere

Same password for multiple accounts

    CHUNK 2: WHY PASSWORD REUSE IS DANGEROUS

    TEACHER CHUNK
    15 mins

    The Domino Effect of Shared Passwords

    Imagine if you used the same key for your house, your school locker, your bike lock, and your safe. If someone stole that key, they'd have access to everything! Using the same password everywhere creates the same problem.

    1

    What Are Data Breaches?

    A data breach happens when hackers break into a company's computers and steal user information, including passwords.

    Companies Store Your Data

    When you create an account, companies store your username, email, and password in their databases.

    Hackers Attack Databases

    Criminals try to break into these databases to steal personal information and passwords.

    Your Information Gets Stolen

    If the attack succeeds, hackers now have your login information for that website.

    2

    Checking if Your Data Has Been Stolen

    There's a helpful website that can tell you if your email address has been involved in any known data breaches.

    Have I Been Pwned?

    A free service that tells you if your email has been in a data breach

    Visit Have I Been Pwned

    What It Does

    • Searches billions of breached accounts
    • Shows which websites were hacked
    • Tells you what information was stolen
    • Explains when the breach happened
    • Helps you know which passwords to change

    Important Safety Notes

    • Only enter email addresses you own
    • Use it to stay informed, not to worry

    Remember: Being in a data breach doesn't mean you did anything wrong! It means a company's security failed. The important thing is knowing about it so you can protect yourself by changing passwords.

    3

    CHUNK 3: PROTECTION STRATEGIES

    TEACHER CHUNK
    10 mins

    Building Your Digital Defense System

    Now that you understand the risks, let's learn how to protect yourself with smart password strategies and tools that make security easier to manage.

    1

    The Golden Rules of Password Security

    1

    UNIQUE EVERYWHERE

    Every account gets its own password - no sharing allowed!

    2

    LONG & STRONG

    12+ characters with letters, numbers, and symbols

    3

    UNPREDICTABLE

    Avoid personal information and common patterns

    4

    UPDATED REGULARLY

    Change passwords if there's a security breach

    2

    Password Managers: Your Digital Assistant

    A password manager is like having a super-secure digital notebook that creates and remembers unique passwords for all your accounts.

    What Password Managers Do

    • Generate super strong passwords automatically
    • Store all your passwords securely
    • Fill in login forms for you
    • Work on all your devices
    • Alert you about data breaches
    • Check for weak or reused passwords

    Popular Password Managers

    Built-in Options:

    Browser password managers (Chrome, Safari, Firefox)

    Dedicated Apps:

    1Password, Bitwarden, LastPass

    Always ask a parent:

    Before setting up any password manager

    3

    Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

    2FA adds an extra layer of security by requiring two things to log in: your password AND something else (like a code sent to your phone).

    Something You Know

    Your password

    Something You Have

    Your phone or device

    Extra Security

    Much harder to hack!

    Enable 2FA on important accounts like email, social media, and gaming platforms whenever possible!
